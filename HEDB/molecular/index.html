Fundamental Molecular Processes in Development

Six categories of molecules guide embryonic development:
Transcruiption Factors: are proteins possessing domain that bind to the DNA of promoter or rnhance regions of specific genes
Signaling Molecules: leave the cells that produce them and exert their effects on others
Receptor Molecules: are transmembrane proteins prodtuding through the plasma membrane
Signal Transducting Pathway: transmits molecular signal to the nucleus of the responding cell
Small RNA
Retinoic Acid

Category 1 Transcription Factors

A transcription factor molecule binds to the DNA at its binding site, and thereby regulates the production of a protein from a gene.

Based on their structure and how they interact with DNA, four groups is listed here:

  • Homeobox
  • T-Box gene family
  • Helix-Loop-Helix Transcription Factors
  • Zinc finger transcription factors

Homeobox-Containing Genes and Homeodomain Proteins

Homeodomain proteins: contain a highly conserved homeodomain of 60 amino acids with helix-loop-helix structure.

Homeobox: The 180 nucleotides in the gene that encode the homeodomain.

Organization of the human HOX complex

Paralogous group: The genes or clusters of genes at different chromosomal locations in the same organism that have structural similarities indicating that they derived from a common ancestral gene and have since diverged from the parent copy oby mutation and selection or drift.

Human possess 39 homeobox genes on the 4 chromosomes in 13 paralogous groups.

Genes on the 3’ ends of each of the complexes are expressed earlier and more anteriorly than those on the 5’ end.

Pax gene family

CNS, Central nevous system; KO, knockout

T-Box Family

T-box(Tbx) genes

  • Structure: coding 180-200 amino acids for DNA binding (18 genes in human genome)
  • Function: evolutionarily ancient, in development

Basic Helix-Loop-Helix Proteins

  • Structure:
  • Function: regulation of embryonic development, particularly in neurogenesis, myogenesis, heart development and hematopoiesis.

Forkhead(Fox) Gene family

Zinc Finger Transcription Factors

The zinc finger family of tanscription fators consists of proteins with regularly placed cystidine and histidine units that are bound by zinc ions to cause the polypeptide chain to puncker into fingerlike structures. There “fingers” can be inserted into specific regions in the DNA helix.

Sox Genes: High-mobililty group (HMG) domain

  • Structure: Sox proteins forming complexes with other transcription factors
  • Function: Affected cell type

WT1

  • Structure: Proline and glutamine-rich domain with four zinc fingers
  • Function: Formation of both the kidney and gonads.

Categoriy 2 Singnaling Molecules

  • Embryonic development proceeds on the basis of chemical signals sent from one group of cells and received and acted on by another.
  • The same signaling molecule can be used at many different times and places.
  • The concentration or duration of exposure to a signaling molecule are important determinants of the fate of group of responding cells.
  • The signaling moleculrs are members of large families.
    • Transforming growth factor - beta family
    • Fibroblast growth factor family
    • Hedgehog family
    • Wnt family
    • Other actions of signaling molecules

Transforming Growth Factor-β Family

Structure and the activation of the growth factor, TGF-β1.

  1. The newly synthesized peptide consists of a C-terminal bioactive region, to which is attached a long glycosylated proregion and an N-terminal signal sequence.
  2. The proregion is cleaved off from the bioactive region, and two secreted bioactive regions form a dimer that is maintained in a latent form by being complexed with the separated proregions.
  3. Through an activation step, the bioactive dimer is released from the proregions and can function as a signaling molecule.

Fibroblast Growth Factor Family

  • The FGF family consists of 23 members, each of which has distinctive functions.
  • The FGF involved in angiogenesis, wounding healing, and embryonic development.
  • The FGF must bind to heparan sulfate in ECM to active their receptors.
  • The FGF receptors are expressed at the cell surface of target cells.

Hedeghog Family

The sonic hedgehog (shh) signaling pathway.

  1. The signaling peptide is cleaved off the newly synthesized polypeptide, and the remainder undergoes glycosylation;
  2. The remaining peptide undergoes autocleavage under the influence of the C-terminal portion, and cholesterol binds to the N-terminal part, which is the active part of the molecule;
  3. The N-terminal part is secreted and bound to the cell surface;
  4. The bound shh molecule is released from the cell surface throuth the action of a product of dispersed (disp);
  5. The released shh inhibits the inhibitory effect of Patched on smoothened;
  6. On released from the inhibitory influence of Patched, smoothened is transported to the membrane of the primary cilium;
  7. Releases the transcription factor Gli from a complex of molecules bound to microtubules;
  8. Gli enters the nucleus and binds to the DNA, influencing the experession of many genes. Lower left: When not bound to shh, Patched inhibits the activity of smoothened, and Gki, in its repressor form GliR, goes to the nucleus and acts as a transcriptional repressor.

Wnt Family

  • The Wnt family is complex, with 18 members represented in the mouse.
  • In mammals, Wnts play many important roles during the period of gastrulation.
  • Later in development, Wnts are involved in cellular differentiation and polarity.

Other Actions of Signaling Molecules

  • Some signaling molecules are positive regulators of growth (shh and FGF family).
  • Others serve as negative regulators of growth (some member of BMP falimy).
  • Some signaling molecules (chordin, noggin, and gremlin) inhibit the activity of inhibitor (BMP).

Category 3 Receptor Molecules

Two typrs of receptors:

Cell surface receptor:

  • Ligands: big, hydrophilic, amino acids, peptide, protein.
  • Types: 1. receptor with intrinsic protein kinaes activity; 2. receptors that use a second messenger system.

Intracellular receptor:

  • Ligands: small, hydrophobic, steroids, thyroid hormone.
  • Types: 1. receptors are in the cytoplasm; 2. receptors are in the nucleus.
