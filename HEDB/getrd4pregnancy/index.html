Gametogensis

Gametogensis has 4 phases:

  • The germ cells which is extraembronic origined migrate into the gonads. This process is same for both male and female.
  • Mitosis of the germ cells, making an increase in the number.
  • Meiosis of the germ cells, making a reduction in chomosomal number.
  • Structural and functional maturation of the eggs and spermatozoa.

Phase 1 Migration

  1. Precursors of gametes arise outside the gonads;
  2. These cells can be recongnizable at 24d after fertilization;
  3. These precursors are located in the endodermal layer of the yolk sac
  4. They have characteristics as below:
    • Large size,
    • High content of glycogen,
    • Hign content of alkaline phosphatase.

The process of Migration:
- Germ cells exit from the yolk sac
- Get into the hindgut epithelium
- Migrate through the dorsal mesentery
- Reach the primordia of the gonads

If primordial germ cells lodge in extragonadal sites and survived, they become teratomas.

Quiz

1, When does meiosis begin in the female and in the male?

In the female, meiosis begins before or at birth. In the male, meiosis begin after puberty.

  1. What is the difference between spermatogenesis and spermiogenesis?

Spermatogenesis means the whole process of the meiosis and the cellular preparation.

Spermiogenesis means a part of spermatogenesis which is the process of transformation from spermatids to spermatozoa.

  1. At what two stages of oogenesis in meiosis arrested in the female?

The first stage is from the birth to the puberty. The meiotic division arrests in diplotene state.

The second stage is after ovulation and before the fertilization. The meiosis arrest at metaphase II.
